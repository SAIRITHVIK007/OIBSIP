Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 - 27 July 2015) was a revered Indian aerospace scientist, statesman, and author. Widely regarded as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in the country's missile development programs, Dr. Kalam also served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Dr. Kalam's scientific career blossomed at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He played a leading role in developing India's indigenous missile capabilities and space exploration programs. His vision and leadership were instrumental in the success of projects like the SLV-III, India's first satellite launch vehicle.
